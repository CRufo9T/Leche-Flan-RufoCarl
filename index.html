Leche Flan

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 10 large egg yolks
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract or lemon zest (optional, for added flavor)

Instructions

  1. Step 1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves. Stop stirring and let the mixture boil until it turns into a golden amber color. Quickly pour the caramel into a llanera (traditional flan mold) or a round baking dish. Tilt the mold to evenly coat the bottom. Set aside to cool and harden.
  2. Step 2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk the egg yolks gently to avoid creating too many bubbles. Add the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract or lemon zest. Mix until smooth and well combined. Do not overmix to avoid air bubbles in the custard
  3. Step 3. Strain the custard mixture through a fine sieve into the caramel-lined mold. This step ensures a silky texture. Cover the mold tightly with aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in during steaming or baking.
  4. Step 4. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the mold in a larger baking pan. Add hot water to the larger pan to create a water bath (bain-marie). Bake for about 50-60 minutes, or until the flan is set and slightly jiggly in the center.
  5. Step 5. Allow the flan to cool to room temperature. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results. To serve, run a knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it. Invert onto a serving plate, allowing the caramel to coat the top.
